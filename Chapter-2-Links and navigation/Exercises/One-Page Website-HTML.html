About | Asteroid City

Asteroid City (or "Asteroid City") is a 2023 American comedy-drama film written, directed, and produced by Wes Anderson, from a story he wrote with Roman Coppola. Its plot is mostly concerned with a play of the events of a Junior Stargazer convention in a retrofuturistic version of 1955, but it becomes metatextual because the making of the play is the subject of a television documentary. It is Anderson's homage to popular memory and mythology about extraterrestrials and UFOs witnessed in the American Southwestern desert in close proximity to atomic test sites during the postwar period of the 20th century.

Plot: Summarized

We open the film with a television host discussing a celebrated playwright and his fictional play, Asteroid City.

The movie transitions into the colorful world of the play, thrusting us into Asteroid City where war photojournalist Augie, accompanied by his son and three daughters, arrives at an astronomy convention. After a car breakdown and a tense phone call with his father-in-law, Augie struggles with revealing the truth about his late wife.

At the convention, Augie's family meets the famous actress Midge and her daughter, hinting at potential romantic entanglements. Meanwhile, a parallel storyline unfolds in the real world, revealing insights into the playwright's intentions and casting choices. Drama ensues as relationships develop, casting decisions are made, and conflicts arise, including an uncertain actor and a disgruntled actress whose scene is cut.

The convention takes a chaotic turn when a groovy alien disrupts a military general's speech, stealing a crucial meteorite and triggering a quarantine. News of scandalous affairs spreads, further complicating matters and frustrating the general's attempts to lift the quarantine.

Inspired by the ingenuity of nerdy science kids, the community rises up against military authority, using their inventions to overcome obstacles and defeat the military.

Tragedy strikes as the playwright passes away, and an epilogue reveals the eventual departure of Augie's family from Asteroid City after the quarantine is lifted.

The People's Opinions About The Movie:

"Wes Anderson's latest whimsical creation, Asteroid City, is a delightful and heartwarming coming-of-age story set in the nostalgic backdrop of a 1950s summer camp. With his signature aesthetic and quirky characters, Anderson weaves a colorful tapestry of childhood adventures, touching relationships, and laugh-out-loud humor."

"This is not Anderson's best work, not by a long shot. It's nowhere near Rushmore, Grand Budapest or Life Aquatic. However, it still has that dry Wes Anderson humor we have come to know and love. It's not the characters or dialogue that make this movie quite average, it's the confusion and lack of substance that drop this one down a notch."

"This is a wonderful story within a play within a film. It's really a love note to actors who love Wes Anderson, in my opinion. Maybe it's not for general movie audiences...but I think it's great that he got an all-star blockbuster cast to do a film that's not necessarily meant to please EVERYONE."

"Wes Anderson reminds me of writers who focus more on style than substance. I realize I'm making a very broad brushed statement about a master craftsman with a very unique world vision. However, he has repeatedly been disappointing me with his stories lately, so I couldn't resist the dig. He makes breathtakingly pretty pictures straight out of dreams but like dreams, the narrative is sometimes not very coherent."
